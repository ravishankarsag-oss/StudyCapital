How to Get an Education Loan for the USA — Step by Step

1

Get Your I-20 / Offer Letter

You need a conditional or unconditional offer letter from a US university to apply for an education loan. The I-20 (for F-1 students) is required for visa — the loan sanction letter helps in visa interviews.

2

Free Consultation with StudyCapital

Share your university name, course, and fee structure with us. We identify the 2–3 best lenders for your profile and present all terms side by side.

3

Document Preparation & Application

We help you prepare the complete document set — admission letter, I-20, KYC, income proof, fee structure — and submit to the chosen bank.

4

Sanction Letter (48–72 hours)

Receive your loan sanction letter. This is a key document for your F-1 visa interview — it demonstrates financial capability to the US consulate.

5

Forex Remittance to the USA

After visa approval, we assist with remitting tuition fees to the US university in USD at competitive exchange rates through RBI-authorised forex partners.

F-1 Visa

Education Loan & Your F-1 Visa — What You Need to Know

📄

Sanction Letter for Visa Interview

A bank sanction letter proves financial support at your F-1 visa interview. StudyCapital helps you get it fast — often before your I-20 arrives.

💱

Disbursement After Visa

Banks typically disburse the loan after you receive your F-1 visa. The first disbursement covers the first semester tuition, remitted directly to the university.

🔄

Subsequent Disbursements

Later semesters' fees are remitted each semester upon submission of your enrollment confirmation. Living expense components are disbursed monthly or per semester.
