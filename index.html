Frequently Asked Questions — Education Loans India 2026

Everything you need to know about education loans in India and abroad.

Education loan interest rates in India start from 8.5% per annum for government banks like SBI, Bank of Baroda, and Union Bank. Private banks like HDFC Credila and Axis Bank typically offer rates between 10–13% p.a. StudyCapital compares rates across 50+ lenders to find you the lowest rate based on your profile, loan amount, and destination country.

Most banks in India offer collateral-free education loans up to ₹7.5 lakhs under the IBA model. For loans above ₹7.5 lakhs (up to ₹75 lakhs), collateral such as property, FD, or LIC policy may be required. Private lenders like HDFC Credila and Prodigy Finance offer higher unsecured loans based on your university's ranking and co-applicant income.

The moratorium period in an education loan is the grace period during which you are not required to pay EMIs — typically the course duration + 6 to 12 months after course completion or getting a job, whichever is earlier. During this period, simple interest accrues which can optionally be paid to reduce overall interest burden.

Yes! StudyCapital facilitates education loans for 50+ countries including USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Ireland, France and more. For Canada, we also assist with GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) requirements. Loans cover tuition, accommodation, travel, study materials, and living expenses abroad.

Under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act, the interest paid on education loans is fully deductible from taxable income for up to 8 consecutive years from the year repayment begins. There is no upper limit on the deduction amount. This applies to loans taken for higher education in India or abroad.

Going to a single bank means you see only their rates and products.
