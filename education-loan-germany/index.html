Blocked Account Explained

What Is a Blocked Account (Sperrkonto) for Germany?

🏦

What Is a Blocked Account?

A blocked account (Sperrkonto) is a mandatory German bank account holding €11,208 — the official proof you can support yourself for one year in Germany. Required for German student visa.

💡

Who Provides It?

Fintiba and Expatrio are the two DAAD-approved online providers. Traditional German banks like Deutsche Bank also offer blocked accounts. StudyCapital guides you on the best option.

✅

Include It in Your Loan

The €11,208 blocked account amount (≈₹10.2 lakhs) can be included in your education loan. StudyCapital coordinates the remittance to Fintiba/Expatrio directly from your loan.

🇩🇪 Germany is tuition-free! Most public German universities charge only a semester contribution of €150–400. Your loan mainly covers the blocked account, accommodation (₹5–8 lakhs/year) and living expenses (₹4–7 lakhs/year).
