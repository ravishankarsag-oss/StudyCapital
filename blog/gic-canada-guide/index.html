GIC for Canada Student Visa — Complete Guide 2026

The Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is mandatory for most Indian students applying for a Canadian study permit. This guide explains what it is, how much you need, the best providers, and how to include it in your education loan.

📅 May 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ StudyCapital Advisors

What Is a GIC for Canada?

A Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC) is a Canadian bank investment that guarantees your deposited funds will be returned with interest over a set period. For immigration purposes, a GIC serves as proof of financial support — showing IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) that you have enough funds to support yourself during your studies in Canada.

The GIC is purchased from a IRCC-approved Canadian financial institution before arriving in Canada. Once you arrive, the funds are released to you in monthly instalments to cover living expenses.

🇨🇦 2026 GIC Requirement: As of 2025, Canada requires students to show CAD 20,635 (approximately ₹12.8–13.5 lakhs) as living expense proof — in addition to tuition fees. The GIC covers this living expense requirement. Always verify the latest amount at canada.ca before applying.

Who Needs a GIC for Canada?

The GIC is required for students applying under the Student Direct Stream (SDS) — a faster visa processing track available to students from India, China, Philippines, Vietnam and several other countries.

  • SDS applicants (recommended): GIC is mandatory. Processing time is 20 days vs 60+ days under the regular stream.
  • Regular stream applicants: GIC is not mandatory, but you must show equivalent funds through bank statements (6 months) or an education loan sanction letter.
💡 StudyCapital Recommendation: Always apply through SDS if you qualify. The faster 20-day processing significantly reduces your stress and timeline. A GIC is a small price to pay for this advantage.

How to Open a GIC — Step by Step

1

Choose a GIC Provider

Select from the IRCC-approved list above. CIBC is most popular with Indian students for speed and ease.

2

Apply Online from India

All providers allow online account opening from India before you arrive. You'll need your passport, admission letter and personal details.

3

Transfer CAD 20,635 from India

Remit the GIC amount from India via your bank or forex provider. This can come from your education loan (StudyCapital arranges the remittance directly).

4

Receive GIC Certificate

Once funds are received and verified, the bank issues a GIC certificate (usually within 2–5 business days). This certificate goes into your study permit application.

5

Include in Visa Application

Upload the GIC certificate in your IRCC study permit application as proof of financial support.

6

Activate on Arrival in Canada

Once you land in Canada, activate your GIC account at the bank. Funds are released in monthly instalments (approx. CAD 1,720/month) to your Canadian bank account.

Can I Include GIC in My Education Loan?

Yes. The GIC amount (CAD 20,635 ≈ ₹12.8–13.5 lakhs) can be included as part of your total education loan amount when applying through StudyCapital. We coordinate the remittance directly from your loan to the Canadian bank — so you don't need to arrange funds separately.
